🎧 Steamify — Subscription Revenue & Retention Analysis
📌 Business Context

Steamify is a subscription-based digital streaming platform offering multiple plans including Basic, Premium, Duo, Student, and Annual. As the platform scales, leadership wants to understand subscription health, retention dynamics, and long-term revenue sustainability.

This analysis evaluates growth trends, lifecycle behavior, plan-level economics, and expansion efficiency to identify where performance is weakening and how to restore sustainable expansion.

🎯 Objective

This analysis aims to determine:

Whether subscription growth is sustainable
Where customers disengage across the lifecycle
Which plans drive long-term value
How upgrades, downgrades, and churn affect revenue
What interventions can improve retention and growth efficiency
📊 Executive Summary — Steamify Subscription Performance

Between 2023 and 2024, Steamify shifted from growth to contraction. Paid users declined from 28K to 19K (-32%), while ARPU increased from $18.8 to $20.5 (+9%), indicating pricing improvements partially offset user loss. However, Net Revenue Retention fell from 102.7% to 99.2%, confirming churn now exceeds expansion revenue.

Revenue leakage widened significantly. Approximately $298K in revenue was lost, while new MRR contributed only ~$9K, creating a 33× gap between revenue lost and revenue gained. Although onboarding improved, long-term retention weakened, with M11 retention declining from 60% to 42%.

Trial conversion declined from 7.2% to 6.0%, new paid users remained flat (~545), and downgrade activity increased, further weakening expansion revenue. These trends indicate Steamify is not facing demand issues but retention inefficiencies.

🔍 Key Insights
💰 Revenue Sustainability

Priority: Critical

Subscriber growth reversed, declining from 28K (2023) to 19K (2024) while ARPU increased. Pricing improvements temporarily masked churn but failed to stabilize revenue. Net Revenue Retention falling below 100% confirms contraction.

Monthly recurring revenue increased in 2023 from ~218K to ~520K, but declined in 2024 from ~510K to ~379K, indicating structural deterioration. Revenue lost increased from 212K to 298K, widening the gap between churn and acquisition.

Month-over-month MRR growth also turned consistently negative in 2024, confirming the decline is structural rather than seasonal.

🔁 Retention & Churn Behavior

Priority: Critical

Early lifecycle churn improved from 26% to 6%, suggesting onboarding improvements were effective. However, long-term retention weakened significantly:

M1 retention: 77% → 65%
M6 retention: 63% → 51%
M11 retention: 60% → 42%

Lifecycle analysis shows 67.75% of churn occurs within the first two renewals, indicating users adopt successfully but fail to build sustained engagement.

📉 Plan Economics & Value Drivers

Priority: High

Basic plans generate the highest revenue (~$1.9M) but rely on volume rather than value. Premium plans contribute strong revenue yet show the highest churn (~6.5%). Basic churn (~5.49%) and Duo (~4.59%) are comparatively lower, reinforcing risk concentration in higher tiers.

Annual plans demonstrate the strongest retention (~1% churn) and stable revenue. Duo plans deliver the highest lifetime value (~$2,663), while Student plans show the lowest (~$421).

Revenue is concentrated in higher-risk tiers while stable high-LTV plans remain underutilized.

🚀 Growth & Conversion Efficiency

Priority: Medium

Trial acquisition remained stable (~1.4K–1.5K users), indicating sustained interest. However, trial-to-paid conversion declined from 7.2% to 6.0%, reducing new subscriber inflow. New paid users (~545) remained insufficient to offset churn.

Despite consistent trial volume, new MRR (~9K) remains significantly lower than revenue lost (~298K).

🔄 Expansion vs Contraction

Priority: Medium

Upgrade rates declined while downgrade rates increased, indicating migration toward lower-tier plans. Plan migration shows users moving from Premium and Duo toward Basic and Student tiers, reducing ARPU and weakening expansion revenue.

Expansion revenue weakened, with new MRR (~9K) significantly lower than revenue lost (298K). This imbalance confirms expansion can no longer compensate for churn.

🎯 Strategic Recommendations
1. Prioritize Mid-Lifecycle Retention to Restore Growth

Steamify’s primary issue is declining long-term engagement rather than onboarding. Early churn improved (26% → 6%), but retention dropped significantly later (M11 60% → 42%). With 67.75% of churn occurring early, users fail to build sustained usage.

Improving mid-lifecycle engagement will increase customer lifetime value and move Net Revenue Retention back above 100%.

2. Stabilize Premium Plan to Protect High-Value Revenue

Premium generates strong revenue but has the highest churn (~6.5%). Increasing downgrades suggest insufficient perceived value. Improving feature differentiation will reduce churn and stabilize revenue.

3. Improve Acquisition Quality Toward High-Retention Plans

Basic plans dominate volume, but Annual and Duo plans show stronger retention and higher lifetime value (~$2,357 and $2,663). Increasing adoption of these plans improves revenue stability.

4. Rebuild Expansion Engine to Offset Churn

Upgrade rates declined while downgrades increased. New MRR (~9K) is far below revenue lost (~298K), confirming expansion is no longer compensating churn.

Encouraging upgrades and reducing downgrades will improve Net Revenue Retention and support sustainable growth.
